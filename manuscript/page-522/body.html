She ended heer, or vehement despaire Broke off the rest; so much of Death her thoughts Had entertaind, as di’d her Cheeks with pale. But Adam with such counsel nothing sway’d, To better hopes his more attentive minde Labouring had rais’d, and thus to Eve repli’d.

Eve, thy contempt of life and pleasure seems To argue in thee somthing more sublime And excellent then what thy minde contemnes; But self-destruction therefore saught, refutes That excellence thought in thee, and implies, Not thy contempt, but anguish and regret For loss of life and pleasure overlov’d. Or if thou covet death, as utmost end Of miserie, so thinking to evade The penaltie pronounc’t, doubt not but God Hath wiselier arm’d his vengeful ire then so To be forestall’d;
