Thus talking hand in hand alone they pass’d On to thir blissful Bower; it was a place Chos’n by the sovran Planter, when he fram’d All things to mans delightful use; the roofe Of thickest covert was inwoven shade Laurel and Mirtle, and what higher grew Of firm and fragrant leaf; on either side Acanthus, and each odorous bushie shrub Fenc’d up the verdant wall; each beauteous flour, Iris all hues, Roses, and Gessamin Rear’d high thir flourisht heads between, and wrought Mosaic; underfoot the Violet, Crocus, and Hyacinth with rich inlay Broiderd the ground, more colour’d then with stone Of costliest Emblem: other Creature here Beast, Bird, Insect, or Worm durst enter none;
