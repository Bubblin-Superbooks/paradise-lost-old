But say, what mean those colourd streaks in Heavn, Distended as the Brow of God appeas’d, Or serve they as a flourie verge to binde The fluid skirts of that same watrie Cloud, Least it again dissolve and showr the Earth?

To whom th’ Archangel. Dextrously thou aim’st; So willingly doth God remit his Ire, Though late repenting him of Man deprav’d, Griev’d at his heart, when looking down he saw The whole Earth fill’d with violence, and all flesh Corrupting each thir way; yet those remoov’d, Such grace shall one just Man find in his sight, That he relents, not to blot out mankind, And makes a Covenant never to destroy The Earth again by flood, nor let the Sea Surpass his bounds, nor Rain to drown the World With Man therein or Beast; but when he brings Over the Earth a Cloud, will therein set His triple-colour’
