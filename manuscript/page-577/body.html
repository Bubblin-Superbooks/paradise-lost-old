Then with uplifted hands, and eyes devout, Grateful to Heav’n, over his head beholds A dewie Cloud, and in the Cloud a Bow Conspicuous with three lifted colours gay, Betok’ning peace from God, and Cov’nant new. Whereat the heart of Adam erst so sad Greatly rejoyc’d, and thus his joy broke forth.

O thou that future things canst represent As present, Heav’nly instructer, I revive At this last sight, assur’d that Man shall live With all the Creatures, and thir seed preserve. Farr less I now lament for one whole World Of wicked Sons destroyd, then I rejoyce For one Man found so perfet and so just, That God voutsafes to raise another World From him, and all his anger to forget.
